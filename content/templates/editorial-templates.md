# YOLKMEET Editorial Templates

These templates define the launch-batch article formats used by Task 8. They are intentionally operational and citation-led so the site does not publish empty listicles, copied SERP summaries, or unsupported product claims.

## Tool Review
- Quick answer: who should use the tool, who should skip it, and the strongest constraint.
- Source-derived analysis or firsthand test note: clearly state which evidence is official documentation, public pricing, local workflow testing, or an editorial comparison.
- Decision table: best fit, setup effort, pricing-risk note, automation fit, and refresh cadence.
- Practical workflow: one realistic setup path with assumptions.
- Source log: official pages and dated public references.
- Originality checklist: no copied body text, claims tied to sources, no invented screenshots, no fake testing.

## Tool Comparison
- Quick answer: winner by reader scenario, not a universal winner.
- Comparison table: use cases, workflow fit, automation limits, pricing verification, and maintenance risk.
- Selection path: 3-5 decision rules.
- Internal links: at least two related YOLKMEET guides.
- Source log and update policy.

## Alternative List
- Quick answer: best alternative by switching reason.
- Switching matrix: cost pressure, open-source preference, enterprise controls, ease of use, and integration fit.
- Migration checklist: export, permissions, triggers, review gates, and rollback.
- Source log and originality checklist.

## Workflow Tutorial
- Quick answer: the practical workflow outcome.
- Inputs and assumptions: tools, accounts, data shape, limits, and review gate.
- Steps: setup, prompt/run, verification, failure handling, and refresh.
- Evidence note: source-derived unless the workflow was actually executed.
- Internal links and update policy.

## Troubleshooting/Error Fix
- Quick answer: most likely cause and first check.
- Symptom table: symptom, likely cause, test, and fix.
- Safe rollback: how to undo a change.
- When to escalate: vendor status page, billing, permissions, or API quota.
- Source log and update policy.

## Prompt/Template Library
- Quick answer: what the prompt set is for.
- Prompt blocks: role, inputs, constraints, output format, and review criteria.
- QA rubric: factuality, source grounding, tone, length, and policy risk.
- Reuse notes: where to adapt, where not to automate.
- Source log and originality checklist.
